Things (were) recorded.

Fragmented reflections of a self. A Mirror reflection. A captured image _ a captured soul. Footprints in the digital snow. Recorded && forgotten. Maybe so… || maybe only forgotten by me. A ghost in the machine. A [stenographer, documentarian, biographer, spy, stalker, sniper], all silently recording, plodding, calculating, assessing. Who is the [viewer, reader, audience]. Awhile before I combed through the pillars of data, I found a forgotten roll of film in my grandfather’s old camera. It had sat unused on a bookshelf for years. A tool turned in to a monument. A box containing forgotten information. Data, divorced from spatiality and context. Awaiting to be developed and processed. Distorted by the passage of time.

What (is) recorded.

_remember? My mind has been hazy since the virus. Days melt into days melt into days. I barely can account for the time, recollection feels so fragile. Memories, precariously suspended within a damaged system, ready to fall and shatter at a moments notice. Gazing upon the records collected about (me). A better system, it serves up a superior record to my own recollections. What does it mean when a system built of cold machines hold a better commemoration of me; I’m a breathing machine, I have been glitching out of control. My heartbeat speaks an encrypted morse code, a language I cannot speak. Everything is communicating information. Remember?_
